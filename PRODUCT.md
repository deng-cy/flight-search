# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Travelers comparing multi-airport, multi-date trips with a mix of paid cash fares and award availability. They are usually deciding under price pressure and need to quickly understand the tradeoff between cheap, inconvenient flights and higher-priced, more comfortable options.

## Product Purpose

Flight Search turns cached or refreshed cash, Seats.aero, and browser-observed award data into static reports that help choose complete outbound-return trip plans. Success means the report makes the best practical booking path, cheapest observed prices, timing pain, savings, and data-confidence caveats visible without requiring the reader to inspect raw provider output.

## Brand Personality

Clear, pragmatic, evidence-backed. The interface should feel like a careful travel analyst: calm enough for comparison, direct about savings and tradeoffs, and unwilling to hide messy data-quality caveats.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-page dramatics, decorative dashboard clutter, provider-jargon labels, and summary cards that bury price pain behind implementation details. Do not make users infer whether a cheap option is painful, whether a better-timed option costs more, or whether a cash fare is a true two-leg fare versus separately priced one-ways.

## Design Principles

Lead with the money decision: surface cheapest, least-painful cheap, best scored, and best cash prices before detailed exploration.

Make timing scan horizontally: dates, times, route, flight numbers, stops, and duration should line up predictably so users can compare legs without rereading every card.

Show the tradeoff, not just the rank: explain whether savings come with early departures, late arrivals, extra stops, airport switches, or separate bookings.

Keep evidence portable: reports should remain static, self-contained, sortable, and useful without a running app.

Preserve confidence signals: provider gaps, inferred details, and unverified timing should remain visible as warnings rather than silently filtering out rows.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible controls, readable table and card layouts, and reduced cognitive load for dense flight comparisons. Motion should be minimal and nonessential because these reports are primarily decision tools.
